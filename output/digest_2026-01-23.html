80% Multi-omics reveals an association of the gut butyrate-IDO1-tryptophan axis with Yinchenhaotang plus Zexietang-ameliorated NASH in a microbiota-dependent manner.
Liu W, Sha X, Zhao N et al.
Chinese medicine · 2026-01-21
Summary: This study demonstrates that a traditional Chinese medicine formulation (YCHZX) ameliorates NASH through a gut microbiota-dependent mechanism involving enrichment of butyrate-producing bacteria, suppression of colonic IDO1, and enhanced tryptophan metabolism toward indolelactic acid production. Multi-omics analysis revealed that increased indolelactic acid strengthens gut barrier integrity by upregulating tight junction proteins like occludin via AhR activation, ultimately reducing LPS translocation and hepatic inflammation.
Highly relevant to gut barrier research interests as it provides mechanistic insights into butyrate-mediated regulation of tight junction proteins (occludin), bacterial translocation, and LPS-mediated liver inflammation through comprehensive multi-omics approaches that could be applied to biliary atresia studies.

80% Korean red ginseng extract inhibits microplastic translocation via the gut-liver axis by ameliorating alcohol-induced intestinal disruption.
Baek SM, Lee YJ, Yim JH et al.
Journal of ginseng research · 2025-11-29 · Open Access
Summary: This study demonstrates that Korean red ginseng extract (RGE) prevents microplastic accumulation in liver and intestines by protecting intestinal tight junctions, specifically upregulating ZO-1 expression in alcohol-induced gut barrier disruption. Using C57BL/6 mice and Caco-2 cell models, researchers showed RGE treatment reduced microplastic translocation across the intestinal barrier and prevented secondary liver damage including steatosis and inflammation.
Highly relevant to gut barrier research with direct focus on tight junction proteins (ZO-1), intestinal permeability, and gut-liver axis dysfunction. The mechanistic insights into barrier protection could inform therapeutic approaches for biliary atresia patients who also experience compromised intestinal barrier function.

60% Antibiotic exposure exacerbates acute-on-chronic liver failure via gut microbiota imbalance and secondary liver lesion.
Yang S, Wang J, Yang N et al.
Journal of medical microbiology · 2026-01-01
Summary: This study examined 23 ACLF patients with antibiotic exposure versus 46 controls, finding that antibiotic use was associated with worse survival rates and altered gut microbiota composition. Using both human data and rat models, the authors demonstrated that antibiotic exposure led to decreased microbiota diversity, increased pathogenic bacteria (Enterococcaceae, Klebsiella), and more severe hepatitis, suggesting gut dysbiosis as a mechanism for poor outcomes in ACLF.
While this focuses on ACLF rather than pediatric conditions, it provides valuable insights into gut-liver axis mechanisms and microbiota changes that could inform understanding of gut barrier dysfunction in biliary atresia and other pediatric liver diseases.
